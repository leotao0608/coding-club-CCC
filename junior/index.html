Topics Covered in Junior CCC

According to the contest structure outlined by the CEMC (Centre for Education in Mathematics and Computing):

    • Questions 1 & 2
       Typically straightforward, involving basic loops, conditional statements, and simple arithmetic. 

    • Questions 3 & 4
       Introduce more complexity by combining loops, conditionals, and counting logic. 

    • Question 5
       Covers more advanced topics such as recursion, two-dimensional arrays, and efficient or insightful algorithmic
       approaches

For questions 1 to 3, it is usually easy to implement the solutions correctly and earn full marks, while 
questions 4 and 5 tend to be more challenging, especially for beginners. However, with proper implementation,
partial marks are still achievable even on these harder problems.

Each question is worth 15 marks. Normally, you will enter top 25% with a score around 55 and enter top 10%
with a score of around 60 or more.
